The RAD Ledger — Threats, Tricks & Tactics
Real on-ledger behavior. Real protection. Free education.

1. Partial Payment Manipulation Attempts

One of the oldest XRPL games. Attackers try to send a tiny amount of XRP but spoof a massive “instructed delivery” amount in hopes something misreads it. RAD ignores this garbage instantly.

2. Fake Pathfinding & Dust Probes

Scanners use dust-value sends, incorrect paths, and route probing to see if any token, mint, or AMM has a weak spot. RAD logs it, rejects it, and archives it.

3. Mint Testing & Probe Transactions

Some actors ping mint wallets with zero-value or misleading routes to see if anything triggers. A desperate way to find weak automation. RAD shuts this down instantly — nothing fires, nothing leaks.

4. AMM Sniping Attempts

New pools attract opportunists looking for mispriced entries. They test execution paths, liquidity depth, and timing windows. RAD pools are hardened: no misfires, no free entries, no slip-throughs.

5. Disappearing Wallet Behavior

Attackers often vanish from explorers after a failed attempt, hoping no one logs the transaction. The ledger logs everything — and RAD captures it.

6. OTC Bait & Replay Attempts

Scammers eventually try spoofed TXIDs, fake refunds, or replayed payment attempts. RAD’s OTC system is one-way. Their tricks fail on arrival.
