RAD Ledger — Scam Intel & The Defense Playbook

1. Partial Payment Manipulation Attempts

Attackers spoof huge “instructed delivery” values while only sending dust. They rely on weak code or sloppy validation. RAD rejects this instantly.

2. Fake Pathfinding & Dust Probes

Small-value transactions used to test for mispriced pools, broken AMMs, or unprotected paths. RAD logs every attempt and exposes the route.

3. Mint Testing & Probe Transactions

Attackers ping mints with incorrect data or empty instructions, hoping a misfire produces value. RAD discards these silently.

4. AMM Sniping Attempts

New pools get scanned by bots hunting mispricing. RAD’s liquidity structure and pool logic make these attempts useless.

5. Disappearing Wallet Behavior

When a probe fails, the wallet often hides from explorers. RAD logs the entire chain history and archives it anyway.

6. OTC Replay / Bait Attempts

Scammers try fake TXIDs or refund requests to extract tokens. RAD’s OTC is one-way, immutable, and replay-proof.

7. The RAD Defense Playbook

The most complete XRPL protection guide available. Built for builders, investors, and anyone securing value on-ledger.

7.1 Wallet Architecture

• Separate mint / treasury / reserve / liquidity.
• Use proper flags.
• Disable master keys where safe.
• Harden access flow.

7.2 AMM Defense

• Reject partial payment attempts.
• Harden first liquidity seed.
• Watch dust probes.
• Keep AMM wallet isolated.

7.3 Mint Protection

• Verify tags.
• Reject unknown hits.
• Validate metadata.
• Log all activity.

7.4 Token Defense

• Understand rippling.
• Monitor copycat tickers.
• Track inbound threats.
• Maintain a RAD-style audit log.

7.5 OTC & Presale Defense

• Verify TXIDs.
• Reject fake refund claims.
• Never negotiate OTC.
• Maintain buyer mapping.

7.6 Community Defense

• Teach holders how to verify.
• Publish safe channels.
• Warn about drainer links.
• Implement RAD-style security culture.

The ledger never lies. RAD keeps the receipts.
